What People Can Share

A story does not need to be dramatic to matter. Submissions can be short reflections, longer personal essays, poems, lessons learned, or messages of support for another student who might be going through something similar.

  • Submissions can be anonymous, first-name only, or named.
  • Writers should avoid sharing private details about other people without permission.
  • Stories can focus on body image, academic stress, sports injury, family change, anxiety, identity, recovery, or another student mental health experience.
  • Writers can share what helped, what did not help, what they wish someone understood, or what they are still figuring out.
  • Broad Shoulders Initiative should review every submission before publication and may edit for clarity, length, privacy, and safety.

Broad Shoulders Initiative is not a crisis service, medical provider, or therapy provider. If someone may be in danger or needs urgent support, contact emergency services, a trusted adult, a school counselor, or a licensed professional.
