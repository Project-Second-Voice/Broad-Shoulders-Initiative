Share only what feels safe to share.

You never need to include your full name, school, team, family details, or anything that feels too personal. Broad Shoulders is preparing a form that will support submissions without requiring a Google sign-in.

Story Form

Read this before opening the current story form.

The current Google Form may require you to sign in. Broad Shoulders cannot promise that Google will not associate or collect the email connected to that sign-in, so this form should not be treated as fully anonymous. Broad Shoulders will not display an email address or name with a story unless the contributor clearly gives permission. A no-sign-in form is being prepared before any wider story campaign.

Every submission is reviewed before publication. Publication is not guaranteed, and Broad Shoulders may lightly edit a story for grammar, clarity, formatting, privacy, and safety. When possible, significant edits will be shared with the contributor before publication.

Before submitting, you can read the Privacy & Submission Policy for a calm explanation of review, consent, anonymity, and community standards.

What People Can Share

A story does not need to be dramatic to matter. Submissions can be short reflections, longer personal essays, poems, lessons learned, or messages of support for another student who might be going through something similar.

  • Future no-sign-in forms may support anonymous, first-name-only, or named submissions. The current form may require a Google sign-in.
  • Writers should avoid sharing private details about other people without permission.
  • Stories can focus on body image, academic stress, sports injury, family change, anxiety, identity, recovery, or another student mental health experience.
  • Writers can share what helped, what did not help, what they wish someone understood, or what they are still figuring out.
  • Broad Shoulders Initiative reviews every submission before publication and may edit for clarity, length, privacy, and safety.

Broad Shoulders Initiative is not a crisis service, and submissions are not monitored 24/7. If someone may be in immediate danger, contact emergency services. In the United States, call or text 988, or text HOME to 741741 for Crisis Text Line support.
