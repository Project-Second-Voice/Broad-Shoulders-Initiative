Body Image

Reaching Out

June 17, 20263-4 min readAnonymous Submission

Shared anonymously through the Broad Shoulders Initiative community submissions.

This story discusses body image, panic attacks, family stress, and mental health struggles. Please take care while reading.

Throughout my life, I haven't been the smallest girl in class or sports, and the way people have responded to that has definitely impacted my mental health and my self-confidence. I have also been dealing with a rough family situation, and the intense pressure of school has caused me to have panic attacks almost every night and made me not want to keep going.

However, I have found people who have helped lower those feelings and make me feel as comfortable as possible. I definitely think that being open to talking about your struggles is necessary in order to start your journey to recovery. My friends were definitely an outlet that I always sought out to help me.

Even if you think you don't have someone very close to you, it's important to reach out to someone, even a trusted teacher, adult, or social worker at school.

Resources & Support

If this story resonated with you, these resources may help support individuals struggling with body image, anxiety, family stress, academic pressure, panic attacks, or mental health challenges.

If you are in immediate danger or feel like you may hurt yourself, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room. In the U.S., you can call or text 988 for the Suicide & Crisis Lifeline. You can also text HOME to 741741 to reach the Crisis Text Line.

Teen Line Peer-to-peer emotional support and mental health resources for teenagers facing anxiety, stress, loneliness, family challenges, or emotional hardship. The Jed Foundation Mental health resources focused on emotional wellbeing, anxiety, self-worth, stress management, and support for students and young adults. Mental Health America Mental health education, screening tools, and support resources for individuals navigating anxiety, panic, self-esteem struggles, and emotional distress. The Body Positive Resources focused on body image, self-acceptance, self-worth, and building a healthier relationship with yourself. NAMI San Diego Local mental health support, education, and community resources for individuals and families navigating stress, anxiety, and mental health challenges.
